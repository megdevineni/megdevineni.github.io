Imagining India - India as a narrative of ideas

  • 31 Dec 2016
  • By Meghana Devineni

I've picked Imagining India: Ideas for the new century by Nandan Nilekani as my first book of the year. The sole reason behind the selection being, the book is one of the biggest on my list at 500 pages and I wanted to finish it before my spring semester begins in two weeks.

I included this book on my list as it promises to be a narrative of India in terms of ideas that have shaped the nation over the course of the last 7 decades. Amidst historic financial and political changes in India, I am hoping that this book will provide me with a narrative that spans the ideological history that has shaped independent India.

I've just finished reading the foreword and Nandan Nilekani expertly sets the backdrop for what I can expect from this book. Even though the book is heavily rooted in research and facts, the narrative is simple, forthright and easy to follow. I have high expectations from this book and there's only one way to find out if the book is in fact all that it's touted to be. I'm going to spend this New Year's eve reading about the socialist India of the 70's while thanking heavens for being a 90's child born into the economically liberated nation.
