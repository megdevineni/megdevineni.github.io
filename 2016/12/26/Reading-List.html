2017 Reading List

  • 26 Dec 2016
  • By Meghana Devineni

This list was compiled to include most of my interests and also to push my horizons as a reader. I've tried to challenge myself by including many Non-Fiction books, a genre I haven't explored much. The themes I wanted to read about are human emotions and relationships, design, Japan (I have been meaning to visit/learn more about Japanese culture for a while now), education, feminism, India (need to demonstrate I'm not anti-national because I read about Japan) and starting a company. A few of these books have been on my mind for a few years now and a few have been recommended to me. The list also includes many books I've found through lurking around on Goodreads and Reddit.

The list currently has 35 books. I plan to update the list as the year progresses. The goal for the year is to read 52 books, one per week. I understand this is ambitious, keeping in mind I plan to finish grad school, start my career (cue existential crisis) and you know, maybe have a social life. Reading is something I've enjoyed immensely while growing up and I've failed to make it a part of my life over the past few years. I've had random surges of binge reading once in a while but they were never enough. This reading challenge is my way of attempting to incorporate reading as a long term part of my life.

Wish me luck as I attempt to learn to build bridges while reading about how band-aids were designed and look for a job that lets me decide which concrete pump to pick while reading about what it takes to make a woman president. If it gets too much, I know I have Bukowski to console me, with a side of whiskey of course.
